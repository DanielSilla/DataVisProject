World Happiness Report 2021

A visual exploration into the Gallup World Poll from 2021

Global map of World happiness, taken on a scale from 1-10 with 10 being a utopia and 1 a dystopia





GDP vs Happiness

There is a correlation between a countries' happiness and GDP, though it is a somewhat weak dependancy, as can be seen at the 5.0 happiness scale where GDP ranges from 7 to 10, which on a Logarithmic scale is a difference to the order of 1000.

GDP per capita is in terms of Purchasing Power Parity (PPP), a metric introduced by the World Bank to compare the value of currencies based on the comparitive ability to purchase goods and services

Happiness is measured from a national survey, in which six factors are rated on a scale from 0 to 10; These factors are economic production, social support, life expectancy, freedom, absence of corruption and generosity.






Standard Deviation of Countries' Happiness

The standard deviation is used to display the range of responses citizens of the same country reported. While internally we can see variations in responses rangin up to half a point (see Haiti), these ranges are far less that want we see between countries.
